Background

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Notre Dame, where I work under the guidance of Professor Jane Cleland-Huang. My research focuses on advancing software safety through AI-driven techniques, with particular emphasis on traceability, requirements engineering, and trustworthy AI systems.

Before starting my PhD, I gained valuable industry experience as a Software & Systems Engineer at Lockheed Martin, where I worked on the Aegis System developing Java-based backend controllers and automating system-level tests.

I am passionate about translating research into real-world impact, which led me to co-found SAFA.ai, a startup that bridges the gap between academic research and industry applications in safety-critical systems.
