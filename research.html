Research

My research focuses on advancing software safety through AI-driven techniques, with emphasis on traceability, requirements engineering, and trustworthy systems.

Research Philosophy

My work bridges the gap between cutting-edge AI research and practical applications in safety-critical systems. I believe that AI-driven solutions must not only be technically sound but also transparent, trustworthy, and actionable for practitioners working in high-stakes environments.

Through my research, I aim to create tools and frameworks that enhance software safety while providing the explainability and traceability necessary for human oversight and regulatory compliance.

AI-Driven Traceability

Developing advanced techniques using BERT-based models and Large Language Models (LLMs) to predict missing trace links and automate documentation generation in safety-critical systems.

Key Contributions:

  • Hybrid approaches combining low-resource methods (VSM) with modern LLMs
  • Sentence-transformer embeddings and clustering for LLM context window constraints
  • Automated hierarchical documentation generation from source code

Tools & Technologies:

BERT LLMs Sentence Transformers Vector Space Models

Related Publications:

  • Supporting Software Maintenance with Dynamically Generated Document Hierarchies (2024)
  • ROOT: Requirements Organization and Optimization Tool (2024)

Trustworthy AI Systems

Creating evaluation frameworks for assessing the safety and trustworthiness of AI systems, particularly deep reinforcement learning in Cyber-Physical Systems with focus on small Unmanned Aircraft Systems (sUAS).

Key Contributions:

  • Q&A frameworks for designing trustworthy reinforcement learning systems
  • Evaluation methodologies for RL safety in CPS applications
  • Explainability mechanisms for automated testing processes

Application Domains:

Cyber-Physical Systems sUAS Autonomous Systems Safety-Critical AI

Related Publications:

  • QuestRL: A Q&A Framework for Designing Trustworthy RL Systems (2025)
  • Paper under review at International Conference for AI Engineering
